The tribes of Aquitania, in southwestern Gaul, revolt against Crassus,
sent among them by Caesar, and choose as leaders men who have had
experience in Spain and know the Roman methods of warfare. After
vigorous fighting the enemy’s camp is taken and the revolt crushed.
